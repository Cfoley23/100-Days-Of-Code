Some facts about the breed

  • Originally bred in northeastern Asia by the Chukchi people and kept as companion dogs for their families as well as endurance sled dogs
  • The breed began gaining international noteriety in the early 1900's when they started winning dog sled races
  • Great pack dogs, and get along well with other dogs. Naturally friendly nature makes them an unreliable watchdog.
  • During WWII, the army employed the pups as search and rescue dogs. They were also used for transportation, freighting, and communication.
  • A breed that is known to be cleaner than other breeds with less natural dog odor, but more fur.
